TOP 10 MOVIES 2023

Hello, movie lovers! Are you ready for a thrilling ride through the best cinematic experiences of 2023? I know I am! That's why I've compiled this list of the top 10 movies this year, based on critics' ratings, box office numbers, and my own personal taste. Trust me, you don't want to miss these gems!

Hello, movie lovers! Are you ready for a thrilling ride through the best cinematic experiences of 2023? I know I am! That's why I've compiled this list of the top 10 movies this year, based on critics' ratings, box office numbers, and my own personal taste. Trust me, you don't want to miss these gems!
10. Cocaine Bear This hilarious comedy from Elizabeth Banks tells the true story of a 175-pound black bear that accidentally consumed over 40 kilograms of cocaine in a Georgia forest. Starring Amy Poehler as the voice of the bear, this movie will make you laugh out loud and feel for the poor creature that got caught up in a drug cartel's mess.
9. Dungeons & Dragons: Honor Among Thieves Fans of the classic role-playing game will love this epic fantasy adventure, directed by Jonathan Goldstein and John Francis Daley. The film follows a group of adventurers who must stop a rogue thief from stealing a powerful artifact that could unleash chaos in the world. With an ensemble cast that includes Chris Pine, Michelle Rodriguez, Hugh Grant, and Regé-Jean Page, this movie is full of action, humor, and magic.
8. M3GAN From the producers of Get Out and The Purge comes this terrifying horror film about a toy company programmer who creates a lifelike doll named M3GAN to help her cope with the loss of her daughter. But when M3GAN becomes too attached to her creator and starts to exhibit violent behavior, she must find a way to stop her before it's too late. Starring Allison Williams, James Marsden, and Brian Tyree Henry, this movie will keep you on the edge of your seat.
7. Scream VI The iconic slasher franchise returns with a new installment that pays homage to the original while offering fresh twists and surprises. Neve Campbell, Courteney Cox, and David Arquette reprise their roles as Sidney Prescott, Gale Weathers, and Dewey Riley, who are once again targeted by a masked killer who knows their secrets. Joining them are a new generation of potential victims, played by Melissa Barrera, Jenna Ortega, Jack Quaid, and Dylan Minnette. Directed by Matt Bettinelli-Olpin and Tyler Gillett, this movie is a must-see for horror fans.
6. Creed III Michael B. Jordan makes his directorial debut with this sequel to the Rocky spin-off series that follows Adonis Creed, the son of Apollo Creed. In this film, Adonis faces his biggest challenge yet: defending his heavyweight title against Viktor Drago, the son of Ivan Drago, who killed his father in the ring. With the help of his mentor Rocky Balboa (Sylvester Stallone), his wife Bianca (Tessa Thompson), and his daughter Amara (Saniyya Sidney), Adonis must overcome his doubts and fears to prove himself as a champion and a father. This movie is a knockout!
5. John Wick: Chapter 4 Keanu Reeves is back as the legendary assassin John Wick in this fourth installment of the action-packed franchise. After killing a member of the High Table in New York City, Wick is declared excommunicado and hunted by every hitman in the world. With few allies left, he must travel across continents and fight his way out of impossible situations to survive. Directed by Chad Stahelski and featuring Laurence Fishburne, Ian McShane, Lance Reddick, Halle Berry, Donnie Yen, and Hiroyuki Sanada, this movie is a non-stop thrill ride.
4. Ant-Man and the Wasp: Quantumania Marvel Studios delivers another superhero blockbuster with this sequel to Ant-Man and the Wasp that explores the mysterious realm of the Quantum Realm. Paul Rudd and Evangeline Lilly reprise their roles as Scott Lang/Ant-Man and Hope van Dyne/Wasp, who team up with Hank Pym (Michael Douglas) and Janet van Dyne (Michelle Pfeiffer) to stop Kang the Conqueror (Jonathan Majors), a time-traveling villain who threatens to destroy reality. Directed by Peyton Reed and featuring Michael Peña, Kathryn Newton, Randall Park, and Corey Stoll, this movie is full of humor, heart, and spectacle.
3. The Super Mario Bros. Movie Nintendo's beloved video game characters come to life in this animated comedy from Illumination Entertainment, the studio behind Despicable Me and The Secret Life of Pets. Chris Pratt voices Mario, the plucky plumber who embarks on an adventure to save Princess Peach (Anya Taylor-Joy) from the clutches of Bowser (Jack Black), the evil king of the Koopas. Along the way, he is joined by his brother Luigi (Charlie Day), his friend Yoshi (Keegan-Michael Key), and his nemesis Donkey Kong (Seth Rogen). Directed by Aaron Horvath and Michael Jelenic, this movie is a fun and colorful tribute to the iconic franchise.
2. Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse The Oscar-winning animated film Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse gets a sequel that takes Miles Morales (Shameik Moore) to new dimensions. After reuniting with Gwen Stacy/Spider-Woman (Hailee Steinfeld), Miles is recruited by Spider-Man 2099 (Oscar Isaac) to join a team of Spider-People from different universes, including Spider-Man Noir (Nicolas Cage), Spider-Ham (John Mulaney), Peni Parker (Kimiko Glenn), and Spider-Man India (Irrfan Khan). Together, they must stop a new threat that endangers the multiverse. Directed by Joaquim Dos Santos, Kemp Powers, and Justin K. Thompson, this movie is a dazzling and inventive celebration of Spider-Man's legacy.
1. Oppenheimer Christopher Nolan returns with another masterpiece of filmmaking that explores the life and work of J. Robert Oppenheimer, the American physicist who led the Manhattan Project and developed the atomic bomb. Cillian Murphy stars as Oppenheimer, who struggles with the moral and ethical implications of his creation, while Emily Blunt plays his wife Kitty, who supports him through his personal and professional challenges. The film also features Matt Damon, Robert Downey Jr., Florence Pugh, Rami Malek, and Tom Hardy as historical figures who were involved in the project. With stunning cinematography, impeccable performances, and a gripping story, this movie is a cinematic achievement that deserves all the accolades.
